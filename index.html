Scientific Integrity Disclosure

This project contains two distinct categories of content. Literature-backed elements describe real, characterized organisms and mechanisms with established experimental support (EPA HAPs, CERCLA, ATSDR 2022 sources). Computational / speculative proposals - including the inter-species mercury trafficking cascade and all three novel protein designs (Atx1-Hg, CusA-Hg, CusF-Hg) - are theoretical. They are grounded in structural biochemistry and HSAB principles, but have not been experimentally validated (no in vitro or in vivo testing performed). These elements are clearly labeled throughout.

The Challenge

Complex pollution demands
complex solutions

Thousands of Superfund sites, legacy industrial zones, and agricultural areas hold complex mixtures of heavy metals, solvents, petroleum, pesticides, pharmaceuticals, and more. No single organism can tackle them all.

Chemical remediation often moves contaminants rather than destroying them - transferring the problem from soil to water, or from one phase to another. Bioremediation using living organisms can actually degrade and concentrate pollutants permanently.

The challenge is complexity. Real contaminated sites have multiple pollutant classes co-occurring. This consortium is designed for that reality.

Competitive Advantages

Why multi-kingdom?

Multi-Kingdom Architecture

Fungi (hyphal scaffold + extracellular enzymes) paired with bacteria (metabolic versatility, anaerobic capability) for complementary attack across all contamination zones simultaneously. Neither kingdom alone covers the full pollutant spectrum.

Fungal Highways

Fusarium and Trichoderma hyphae act as physical conduits letting specialist bacteria reach otherwise inaccessible deep zones in contaminated soil - bacteria literally travel along fungal filaments to reach their substrates.

Zero Volatilization Design

All Hg⁰-producing pathways deleted (MIR in M. robertsii; MerA in C. metallidurans). Mercury stays in the system - concentrated, captured, and harvested - rather than escaping as atmospheric vapor.

Consortium Compatibility Engineering

Trichoderma peptaibol synthetase and chitinase genes deleted to stop co-species killing. Penicillium pcb cluster deleted to protect bacterial members. The organisms are engineered to cooperate, not compete.

Novel Protein Designs

Three computationally-derived proteins

These engineered proteins are the novel contribution of this project. Each is designed from structural biochemistry and HSAB (Hard-Soft Acid-Base) principles. None have been synthesized, expressed, or experimentally assayed - they are theoretical proposals.

⚠

No experimental validation. Protein designs are theoretical - derived from structural biochemistry, HSAB principles, and homology to characterized systems. Not yet synthesized, expressed, or assayed.

UNVALIDATED

Atx1-Hg

Hg²⁺ Chaperone - Aspergillus niger
  • MxCxxC → MCxCC motif: third Cys added, spacing reduced - tricoordinate Hg²⁺ binding site
  • Gly spacers allow backbone flexibility for Hg²⁺-preferred linear/trigonal geometry
  • Interaction surface redesigned to dock MT2A instead of native Cu-ATPases
  • Template: MerR C82/C117/C126 coordination geometry (experimentally characterized)
  • Structural basis: ferredoxin-like βαββαβ fold of native M. robertsii Atx1 copper chaperone
UNVALIDATED

CusA-Hg

Transmembrane Efflux Pump - Cupriavidus metallidurans
  • Transmembrane Met relay → Cys (M573C, M623C, M672C) - continuous thiolate channel
  • Creates contiguous soft-donor relay optimized for Hg²⁺'s strong thiolate affinity
  • CusC outer membrane channel unchanged - non-selective for cation identity
  • Basis: HSAB theory - Hg²⁺ (soft acid) has extreme thiolate (soft base) affinity
UNVALIDATED

CusF-Hg

Periplasmic Chaperone - Cupriavidus metallidurans
  • HxMxM → HxCxC: both Met residues replaced with Cys - Hg²⁺-selective from entry point
  • Prevents Cu⁺ competition at the periplasmic capture step before Hg²⁺ reaches CusA
  • Works in tandem with CusA-Hg as a functional unit expressed from synthetic operon
  • Validation approach: E. coli ΔcusA expression, ICP-MS metal export quantification
